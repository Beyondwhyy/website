Energy stored in an Inductor

 
 


 
 

When an electric current is passed through an inductor coil, a magnetic field is established around it. The energy expended by the source in doing so is stored in the form of magnetic field. When the current is decreased, the field surrounding the coil also decreases, causing the stored energy to be returned to the circuit.

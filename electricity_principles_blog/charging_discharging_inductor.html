Charging & Discharging of an Inductor

 
 


 
 

There are several similarities between charging & discharging of a Capacitor to that of an Inductor. A practical inductor has some resistance as well, so the charging circuit can be respresented as shown below.


Consider an inductor that has no energy stored initially. As the switch is closed, the source voltage will appear across the inductor and a current tries to flow through it. But inductor won't allow such an abrupt change in current to take place (Read Inductor). The current will only rise gradually until it reaches its final value (i = V/R). As this happens, simultaneously the Voltage across the inductor falls from the max value (equal to the source voltage) to zero i.e. intially the inductor acts as an open circuit and at steady state it acts as an closed circuit.

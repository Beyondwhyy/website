Charging & Discharging of a Capacitor

 
 


 
 

An uncharged capacitor is similar to a short circuit. Therefore when a battery is connected to the capacitor, there is an initial inrush of current as the battery transports charge from one plate of the capacitor to the other. Then as more and more charges deposit on the plates, it becomes increasingly more difficult for the battery to push more charges onto the plates & as a result the current decreases. As this happens the voltage across the capacitor also increases proportionally.

At a certain point, the maximum no. of charges will accumulate on the plates and correspondingly voltage across the capacitor becomes equal to the supply voltage. Then the current ceases and capacitor acts as an open circuit. In this state the capacitor is said to be charged.

To limit the initial charging current, a resistor is usually connected in series with the capacitor while charging.

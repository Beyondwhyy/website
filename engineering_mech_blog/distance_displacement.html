Distance & Displacement

 
 


 
 

Before you go dive into this section & further, it is expected that you have some understanding of Vectors. If not check out: Vectors

Distance & Displacement are two quantities that are used to indicate the motion of a body. Although they may seem like the same thing, they are two vastly different quantities. Distance is a scalar quantity & Displacement is a vector quantity.

Distance refers to "how much ground an object has covered" during its motion. Whereas Displacement refers to the change in position of an object.



Consider an example (figure above), a person needs to travel from a point A to point B. And he has 3 paths to do this. For each of these paths, the distances are different. Displacement on the other hand solely depends on the starting position and the end position, hence the displacement is same for all the paths.

Now suppose u walk 5m straight, take a U-turn and walk back to your starting point. How much is the distance covered & what is the displacement? The distance covered in this case is 10m. But the displacement is zero. Unlike distance which is always a postive quantity, displacement can be positive, negative or zero.
